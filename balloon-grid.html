A Bird's Eye View

The concept of hot air balloons traces back to the 18th century when the Montgolfier brothers, Joseph-Michel and Jacques-Étienne, invented the first practical hot air balloon. In 1783, they successfully launched a balloon in Annonay, France, using a combination of hot air and a large fabric bag made of paper and silk.

On June 4, 1783, the Montgolfier brothers conducted the first manned flight of a hot air balloon. Their balloon, adorned with a wicker basket, carried passengers Jean-François Pilâtre de Rozier and François Laurent d'Arlandes on a journey that covered about 5 miles and lasted approximately 25 minutes.

[ -] Rapid Advancements: Following the success of the Montgolfier brothers, hot air ballooning quickly gained popularity across Europe. Scientists and inventors began experimenting with different materials and designs to improve balloon technology. In 1785, Jean-Pierre Blanchard and John Jeffries made the first manned balloon flight across the English Channel.
